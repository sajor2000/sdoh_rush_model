üè† What are Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)?

Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) are the conditions where people live, work, and age that affect their health outcomes. These include:

  • Housing: Stable, safe, affordable housing
  • Food: Access to nutritious, affordable food
  • Transportation: Reliable transportation to healthcare
  • Income: Financial resources for basic needs
  • Education: Health literacy and educational opportunities
  • Social Support: Family and community connections

Research shows SDOH account for 80% of health outcomes - more than medical care itself.

üåü Our Vision: Universal SDOH Screening

Our Bridge Strategy: This AI tool helps us prioritize the highest-risk patients while we build capacity for universal screening. Think of it as our pathway to ensuring no patient with urgent social needs gets missed during our transition.
